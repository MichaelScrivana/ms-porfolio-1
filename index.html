02 — 90-Day Vision

How I'd approach
the first 90 days.

Not a wish list — a working plan. Here's how I'd build AI proficiency from baseline to momentum.

01
Days 1–30
Listen, Map, Baseline
Structured interviews across priority functions — marketing, ecomm, supply, medical/PV — to understand where AI is already being used informally and where the biggest friction points are.
Partner with Focus Area Leads and Governance to map the current tool landscape and compliance boundaries (EU AI Act, GDPR, GxP).
Establish a proficiency baseline using Bayer group-level assessment assets — not to rank people, but to identify patterns: which teams are ahead, which are blocked, and why.
Identify 3–5 early adopters already doing interesting work. These become the first peer champions.
02
Days 30–60
Build the Foundation
Launch role-based learning paths for the two highest-impact functions, with hands-on practice built around real workflows — not generic AI courses.
Stand up the first recurring formats: office hours, a monthly clinic, and a simple "use-in-the-flow" guide library that lives where people already work.
Embed compliance into every learning asset from day one — practical guardrails framed as enablers, not blockers. People should leave every session knowing what they can do.
Ship a lightweight adoption dashboard — usage, time saved, content compliance rate. Simple enough that leadership can read it in 60 seconds.
03
Days 60–90
Scale What Works
Expand learning paths to additional functions based on baseline data and early demand signals.
Spotlight early wins — surface the teams using AI well and turn their practices into reusable playbooks that scale across markets.
Close the loop with BU leaders: here's where we started, here's what moved, here's what's next. Build the case for sustained investment.
Begin scoping the peer-to-peer network — not a committee, but a living community of practitioners who teach each other by sharing working examples.

One principle runs through all 90 days: adoption spreads through demonstration, not mandates. Every asset, every session, every dashboard exists to make AI feel less like a corporate initiative and more like a better way to do the work people already care about.
